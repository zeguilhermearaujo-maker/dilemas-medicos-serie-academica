## PART I: THE MASTERS AND THEIR EXERCISES — ADAPTED FOR MEDICINE

### 1.1. Keith Johnstone — Status Exercises for the Clinic

I studied under Keith Johnstone for two consecutive years. These are his exercises, adapted by me for medical training:

| # | Johnstone exercise | Original purpose | Vasconcelos medical adaptation |
|---|-------------------|-----------------|-------------------------------|
| 1 | **Master-Servant** | Explore power dynamics | Physician plays SERVANT to the patient. Learn what happens when the physician voluntarily lowers status: the patient SPEAKS |
| 2 | **Status Transfer** | Shift power mid-scene | Practice raising patient status during consultation. Physical cues: eye contact, sitting below, leaning forward, using patient's name |
| 3 | **Status Number Line** | Players arrange by status 1-10 | Physicians self-assess their status: "What number am I right now?" Target: 4-5 (equal to patient), not 8-9 (the default) |
| 4 | **Toes In/Toes Out** | Low status = toes in, high = out | Body awareness exercise. Physicians film themselves and observe: crossed arms? Standing while patient sits? Behind a desk? |
| 5 | **Breaking Eye Contact** | First to look away = lower status | Practice MAINTAINING eye contact with the patient, not the screen. The screen is the enemy of presence |
| 6 | **Stillness Exercise** | Holding still raises status | Paradox: the physician who is STILL (not rushing, not fidgeting) appears MORE confident AND more accessible |
| 7 | **The Offer** | "Yes, and" — accept every offer | When the patient says something unexpected, the physician does NOT redirect. They ACCEPT and BUILD on it |
| 8 | **Space Control** | High status controls space | Physician becomes aware of how they USE space. Do they stand while the patient sits? Do they block the exit? Do they tower? |
| 9 | **Voice Status** | Tone/volume signals status | Practice speaking at the patient's volume. If they whisper, whisper. If they are loud, match. NEVER above |
| 10 | **The Apology** | Low status apologizes first | The physician who says "I'm sorry this is happening to you" BEFORE diagnosing creates immediate alliance |
| 11 | **Story-Telling Circle** | Each person adds one sentence | Group training: physicians tell a patient's story one sentence at a time — learning to BUILD narrative, not interrupt |
| 12 | **Die on the Third Snap** | Master snaps, servant dies after 3 | Training for recognizing when the SYSTEM (time pressure, screen, bureaucracy) "snaps" at the physician — and how to resist |

### 1.2. Augusto Boal — Forum Theatre for Healthcare

| # | Boal technique | Medical adaptation |
|---|---------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Forum Theatre** | A physician plays a scene: rushed consultation, interrupts at 8 seconds, prescribes without listening. The audience (physicians-in-training) shout "STOP!" and take the physician's place, trying a DIFFERENT approach. They become spect-actors |
| 2 | **Image Theatre** | Physicians create a FROZEN image of "the perfect consultation" with their bodies — without words. Then create "the worst consultation." Compare. What changed? Usually: eye level, distance, touch, openness |
| 3 | **Invisible Theatre** | A trained physician conducts a real consultation using all Vasconcelos techniques WITHOUT announcing it. Colleagues observe. Debrief: "What did you notice that was different?" |
| 4 | **Rainbow of Desire** | For physician burnout. The physician externalizes their conflicting desires: "I want to help" vs. "I want to go home" vs. "I'm afraid of failing." Making the internal visible = first step toward self-care |
| 5 | **Legislative Theatre** | Community health forum where patients and physicians together PROPOSE changes to how consultations are structured. The community becomes co-legislator of their own care |

### 1.3. Viola Spolin — Theater Games for Clinical Focus

| # | Spolin game | Medical application |
|---|------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Mirror exercise** | Physician mirrors patient's body language — building rapport through physical empathy |
| 2 | **Where/Who/What** | Before each consultation: establish WHERE (environment), WHO (this specific person), WHAT (their real concern — not the chief complaint) |
| 3 | **Side-coaching** | Attending physician side-coaches residents DURING consultations: "Slow down. Look at them. What are they NOT saying?" |

### 1.4. Del Close — Group Mind for Healthcare Teams

| # | Close concept | Medical application |
|---|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Group Mind** | The healthcare team functions as ONE organism. When one member detects a pattern, the team responds. No hierarchy in observation |
| 2 | **The Harold** | Long-form improvisation where seemingly unrelated patient complaints are connected over time — training for recognizing SYSTEMIC patterns in health |

### 1.5. Mick Napier — Self-Care First

| Napier principle | Medical adaptation |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| "Take care of yourself first, then your scene partner" | The burned-out physician cannot care for patients. Self-assessment (Maslach) MUST be institutional, not optional |

### 3.2. The 15 Vasconcelos Signs — Complete

| # | Sign | How to observe | What it reveals |
|---|------|---------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Hesitation Sign** | Patient starts, stops, restarts a sentence | What was NOT said is more important |
| 2 | **Door Handle Moment** | Real concern revealed while leaving | High-status environment suppressed the truth |
| 3 | **Partner Glance** | Patient looks at companion before answering | Power dynamics; coercion; dependency |
| 4 | **List Phenomenon** | Written list of 10+ complaints | Anxiety about being heard; low trust |
| 5 | **Medication Display** | Brings all medications unsolicited | Polypharmacy distress; cry for help |
| 6 | **The Euphemism** | "I'm just tired" / "Things are complicated" | Depression, abuse, or suffering coded |
| 7 | **Google Print** | Brings internet research | Fear of something specific |
| 8 | **Chronic Returner** | Same complaint, 3rd+ visit | Previous consultations failed |
| 9 | **Silent Tears** | Crying without words during exam | Total pain (Saunders) |
| 10 | **Refusal Sign** | Refuses exam of specific area | Abuse history, trauma, dysmorphia |
| 11 | **The Minimizer** | "It's nothing, really" for clearly serious symptoms | Fear of diagnosis; denial; need for gentle approach |
| 12 | **Status Surrender** | Patient sits silently, doesn't ask questions, nods at everything | Complete power capitulation; will NOT adhere because never understood |
| 13 | **The Caretaker Mask** | Patient focuses only on THEIR dependent's health, never mentions own | Hidden illness in the caregiver — the most underdiagnosed population |
| 14 | **Phone Shield** | Patient holds phone like shield during entire consultation | Dissociation; distrust; need for PRESENCE protocol |
| 15 | **The Calendar Question** | "How long do I have?" asked calmly | Patient has already processed; needs PLAN, not reassurance |
