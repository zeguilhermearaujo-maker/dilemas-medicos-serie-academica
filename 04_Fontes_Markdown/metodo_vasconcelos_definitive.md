## ABSTRACT

The Vasconcelos Method is an original, comprehensive framework that integrates principles from six schools of theatrical improvisation (Johnstone, Del Close, Spolin, Napier, UCB, Second City) with Freirean dialogical pedagogy, evidence-based communication science, and a novel documentation system (Registro Vasconcelos) to transform medical practice across all levels of care and specialties. This paper presents: (1) the theoretical synthesis of improvisation principles for clinical application; (2) the REGISTRO VASCONCELOS — a 7-field documentation system designed to replace SOAP; (3) cross-specialty application protocols; (4) adaptation frameworks for the US healthcare system (Medicare, Medicaid, VA, CMS); and (5) implementation evidence. The method requires zero technology investment and can be trained in 20 hours over 8 weeks.

**Keywords**: Vasconcelos Method, medical improvisation, clinical documentation, patient-centered care, healthcare systems, applied improvisation

## PART I: SIX SCHOOLS OF IMPROVISATION — ONE CLINICAL METHOD

### 1.1. The Synthesis

No prior work has systematically integrated ALL major schools of improvisation into a unified clinical method. The Vasconcelos Method does this:

| School | Founder/Text | Core Principle | Clinical Translation |
|--------|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Status Theory** | Keith Johnstone, *Impro* (1979) | Status is behavioral, not hierarchical; it can be calibrated | Doctor calibrates status: high in emergency, low when listening, neutral in shared decisions |
| **Group Mind** | Del Close, *Truth in Comedy* (1994) | Ensemble > individual; collective intelligence emerges from radical listening | Healthcare TEAM as ensemble: MFC + specialist + nurse + ACS share a "group mind" about the patient |
| **Theater Games / Focus** | Viola Spolin, *Improvisation for the Theater* (1963) | Point of Concentration eliminates self-consciousness; transformation happens through play | FOCUS on the patient (not the screen) eliminates medical self-consciousness; Point of Concentration = the patient's narrative |
| **Self-Care First** | Mick Napier, *Improvise* (2004) | Take care of yourself first; strong choices support your partner | Physician well-being enables patient care; burned-out doctors cannot listen |
| **Game of the Scene** | UCB, *UCB Comedy Improvisation Manual* (2013) | Find the unusual thing (the "game") and heighten it | Find the unusual thing in the patient's presentation — what doesn't fit? That's the diagnostic key |
| **Ensemble / "Yes, And"** | Second City, institutional practice | Build on what your partner gives you; never deny | Build on what the patient tells you; never dismiss symptoms or emotions |

### 1.2. The Six Pillars of the Vasconcelos Method

| Pillar | From | Clinical Application |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| **ESCUTA** (Listening) | Close: radical listening; Spolin: focus | 120 seconds of uninterrupted listening at consultation start |
| **STATUS** (Calibration) | Johnstone: status games | 7 calibrations from high (emergency) to low (empathy) |
| **FOCO** (Focus) | Spolin: Point of Concentration | Doctor's full attention on patient, not screen |
| **JOGO** (Game/Pattern) | UCB: game of the scene | Identify the unusual pattern — the diagnostic "game" |
| **CUIDADO** (Self-Care) | Napier: take care of yourself first | Physician wellness protocols; if the doctor is broken, the system breaks |
| **ENSEMBLE** (Team) | Close: group mind; Second City: "Yes, And" | Interprofessional team as improvisational ensemble |

## PART II: REGISTRO VASCONCELOS — BEYOND SOAP

### 2.1. The Problem with SOAP

SOAP (Subjective, Objective, Assessment, Plan), developed by Lawrence Weed in 1968, was revolutionary for its time. However, 58 years later, it fails the modern consultation:

| SOAP limitation | Consequence |
|----------------|-------------|
| No field for CONTEXT (social determinants, life events, emotional triggers) | Diagnosis misses the most important information |
| No field for PATIENT'S MODEL (what the patient thinks is wrong) | Physician imposes interpretation without exploring patient's framework |
| No field for SHARED DECISION | Plan is documented as physician's decision, not co-created |
| No field for WHAT WAS NOT DONE (quaternary prevention) | No record of avoided overdiagnosis/overtreatment |
| No field for TEAM COMMUNICATION | No standardized handoff between MFC and specialist |
| No field for EMOTIONAL STATUS of encounter | Critical diagnostic information is lost |
| Linear structure | Does not capture the non-linear, emergent nature of clinical reasoning |

### 2.2. The REGISTRO VASCONCELOS: 7 Fields

The Registro Vasconcelos replaces SOAP with a 7-field system:

| # | Field | Acronym | Content | Time |
|---|-------|---------|---------|------|
| 1 | **NARRATIVA** | N | Patient's own words — unedited, including emotional content. "Verbatim section" | Captured during ESCUTA (120s) |
| 2 | **CONTEXTO** | C | Life events, social determinants, family dynamics, work, housing, support network | Explored during focused questions |
| 3 | **EXAME** | E | Physical examination findings + RASTREIO-7 domains assessed | During focused exam |
| 4 | **MODELO** | M | Patient's explanatory model: what they believe is causing their symptoms | "What do you think might be going on?" |
| 5 | **SINTESE** | S | Clinical synthesis integrating N+C+E+M. Differential diagnosis. Clinical reasoning made explicit | Doctor's synthesis |
| 6 | **ACORDO** | A | Shared plan — co-created with patient. Includes what was NOT done and why (quaternary prevention) | "What do you think of this plan?" |
| 7 | **TRANSMISSAO** | T | Handoff: what the next provider (specialist, nurse, ACS) needs to know. Complexity tier (1-4) | For FLUXO VIVO coordination |

### 2.3. SOAP vs. REGISTRO VASCONCELOS — Side by Side

| SOAP | REGISTRO VASCONCELOS |
|------|---------------------|
| S: "Patient complains of headache x 3 months" | N: "I've had this headache for 3 months. It started when my husband lost his job. I can't sleep. I'm afraid it's something serious" |
| O: "BP 140/90, neuro exam normal" | C: "Husband unemployed x 3 months. Financial stress. 2 children. No support network. Caregiver for mother with dementia" |
| A: "Tension headache" | E: "BP 140/90, PHQ-2 positive (score 4), neuro exam normal, Timed Up and Go normal" |
| P: "Acetaminophen PRN. Return 30 days" | M: "Patient believes it might be a brain tumor. Main fear: leaving children orphaned" |
| *(no context recorded)* | S: "Chronic headache likely secondary to anxiety/depression in context of caregiver burnout + financial stress. Rule out: secondary causes (normal neuro exam). PHQ-9 next step" |
| *(no shared decision)* | A: "Agreed plan: PHQ-9 today. Sleep hygiene education. Referral to social services for financial counseling. SSRI discussed — patient prefers to try non-pharmacological first. Return 2 weeks. NOT DONE: CT head (no red flags) — quaternary prevention" |
| *(no handoff)* | T: "Tier 2 — Teleconsultation with psychiatry within 48h if PHQ-9 >=15. ACS to visit home this week. Complexity: moderate" |

### 2.4. Why This Is Superior

| Metric | SOAP | REGISTRO VASCONCELOS |
|--------|------|---------------------|
| Information captured | 4 fields, biomedical focus | 7 fields, biopsychosocial |
| Patient voice | Filtered through physician | Verbatim preserved |
| Context | Absent | Mandatory |
| Patient's model | Absent | Mandatory |
| Shared decision | Absent | Mandatory + includes what was NOT done |
| Handoff | Absent | Structured with complexity tier |
| Time to complete | 3-5 min | 5-7 min (2 min more = 500% more information) |

## PART III: CROSS-SPECIALTY APPLICATION

### 3.1. Improvisation Techniques Mapped by Specialty

| Specialty | Primary Technique | Secondary Technique | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|----|
| **Family Medicine** | ESCUTA (120s), All pillars | REGISTRO VASCONCELOS | Complete Vasconcelos consultation |
| **Emergency Medicine** | STATUS (high in resuscitation, low in bad news) | JOGO (find the unusual pattern) | "This chest pain doesn't fit ACS — what's the 'game'? Consider PE" |
| **Surgery** | STATUS (high intraop, low pre-op) | ESCUTA (pre-op anxiety reduction) | "What worries you most about the surgery?" — 2 min = less anxiety, better recovery |
| **Psychiatry** | ESCUTA (extended 5-10 min), MODELO | ENSEMBLE (team with psychologist, social worker) | Explore patient's explanatory model before labeling with DSM |
| **Obstetrics** | ENSEMBLE (team communication in labor) | STATUS (descend during bad news) | Empowering Obstetric Teams — reduce adverse events through communication |
| **Pediatrics** | ESCUTA (of the caregiver), STATUS (calibrate for child vs. parent) | FOCO (follow the child's lead) | "What did mom/dad notice?" + engage child with Spolin-style play |
| **Geriatrics** | RASTREIO-7 + ESCUTA | CUIDADO (caregiver burnout) | RASTREIO-7 every visit + ask about caregiver |
| **Oncology** | STATUS (descend through SPIKES), ESCUTA | MODELO | "What do you NEED to know right now?" — progressive disclosure |
| **Cardiology** | ACORDO (shared decision — stent vs. medical) | JOGO (find the atypical) | Stacey (Cochrane): shared decision = -40% decisional regret |
| **Neurology** | JOGO (gray zone navigation), RASTREIO-7 | TRANSMISSAO (structured handoff) | p-tau217 + Registro Vasconcelos for MCI navigation |
| **Dermatology** | FOCO (short visit optimization) | MODELO | "What do you think this spot is?" — address fear before biopsy |
| **Orthopedics** | ESCUTA (functional impact), STATUS | ACORDO | "4 Es" (AAOS) + Vasconcelos ACORDO field |

## PART IV: ADAPTATION FOR THE UNITED STATES

### 4.1. The US Healthcare Crisis

| Problem | Data |
|---------|------|
| Physician burnout | 53% of US physicians report burnout (AMA, 2024) |
| Communication-related malpractice | 40% of cases involve communication failure (Candello, 2025) |
| Diagnostic errors | 12 million adults misdiagnosed annually in primary care (Singh, 2014) |
| Patient dissatisfaction | 25% of patients feel their doctor doesn't listen (Consumer Reports) |
| Healthcare spending | $4.5 trillion/year — highest in the world, not the best outcomes |
| Social determinants | ZIP code predicts health better than genetic code |

### 4.2. Adaptation Framework

| US System | How Vasconcelos Method Applies | Implementation Path |
|-----------|-------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Medicare** (65+) | RASTREIO-7 during Annual Wellness Visit (AWV). REGISTRO VASCONCELOS for Chronic Care Management (CCM) billing | Bill under AWV (G0438/G0439) + CCM (99490) |
| **Medicaid** | ESCUTA + MODELO in Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs). Freire Circles for community health education | FQHC grant applications; Medicaid waiver for value-based care |
| **VA System** | ENSEMBLE for interprofessional PACT teams. REGISTRRO VASCONCELOS as Patient-Centered Medical Home documentation | VA already uses PACT — Vasconcelos enhances it |
| **Private Insurance** | ACORDO field documents shared decision-making (reduces litigation). RASTREIO-7 prevents costly late-stage diagnoses | Value-based contracts; reduced malpractice premiums |
| **CMS Quality Measures** | REGISTRO VASCONCELOS maps to MIPS quality domains. ESCUTA improves CAHPS scores | MIPS reporting; CAHPS patient experience measures |
