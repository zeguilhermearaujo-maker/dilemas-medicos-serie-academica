## DECLARATION OF GENESIS

The Vasconcelos Method originates from the direct teaching lineage of **Keith Johnstone (1933-2023)**, founder of Theatresports, creator of the status theory, and author of *Impro* (1979) and *Impro for Storytellers* (1999). The author of this work, Jose Guilherme Vasconcelos de Araujo, studied under Johnstone for two consecutive years, receiving direct training in status transactions, spontaneity, narrative structure, and the philosophy of human interaction that forms the intellectual foundation of this method.

Johnstone taught that **every human interaction is a negotiation of status** — not of social hierarchy, but of behavioral signals that determine trust, power, and openness. This insight, transplanted to the medical consultation, reveals that the physician's unconscious high-status behavior is a primary barrier to effective care.

**The Vasconcelos Method is the first systematic application of Johnstone's status theory to healthcare,** expanded with principles from Del Close (group mind), Viola Spolin (focus), Mick Napier (self-care), UCB (pattern recognition), Second City (ensemble), and Paulo Freire (dialogical pedagogy).

## PART I: THE PEDESTAL PROBLEM

### 1.1. The Medical Pedestal Is Pathological

The modern physician inherits a pedestal constructed over centuries:

| Historical layer | What it created | What it costs |
|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| Hippocratic tradition | Physician as sacred healer | Impossibility of showing vulnerability |
| 19th century professionalization | Physician as scientist-authority | Patient as object of study, not subject of experience |
| 20th century hospital-centrism | Physician as institutional power | Patient decontextualized from life, family, community |
| Pharmaceutical era | Physician as prescriber | Every complaint becomes a prescription |
| Electronic records era | Physician as data-entry clerk | Eye contact replaced by screen time |

### 1.2. The Cost of the Pedestal (Evidence)

| Consequence | Data | Source |
|------------|------|--------|
| Patients don't tell the truth to high-status doctors | 60-80% of patients withhold information from physicians | Levy et al., 2018, JAMA Network Open |
| Physicians interrupt before patients finish | Mean interruption time: 11-18 seconds | Beckman & Frankel, 1984; Marvel et al., 1999 |
| Patients don't understand discharge instructions | 40-80% of information is forgotten immediately | Kessels, 2003, J Royal Soc Med |
| Patients don't question treatment plans | 85% accept plans they don't fully understand | Britten et al., 2000, BMJ |
| Physicians overestimate their communication skills | Self-assessment has no correlation with actual skill | Davis et al., 2006, JAMA |

### 1.3. Johnstone's Solution: The Voluntary Status Descent

Johnstone discovered that **the most powerful person in the room is the one who can voluntarily lower their status without losing authority.** This is the opposite of what medical culture teaches.

In medicine: A doctor who says "I'm not sure yet — let me think about this with you" is NOT weaker. They are demonstrating the highest form of clinical confidence — **the confidence to not know.**

### 2.3. The Vasconcelos Counter-Protocol: Before You Prescribe

The Vasconcelos Method proposes a mandatory pre-prescription protocol for psychiatric medications in primary care:

| Step | Action | Rationale |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | **ESCUTA 120s**: let the patient tell their story WITHOUT asking about symptoms | The narrative reveals CONTEXT that symptoms alone obscure |
| 2 | **MODELO**: "What do you think is happening to you?" | The patient's own model reveals whether this is distress or disorder |
| 3 | **CONTEXTO**: life events in last 6 months — loss, job, relationship, housing, finances | If there is a CAUSE, this may be normal sadness, not MDD |
| 4 | **PHQ-9 but WITH context**: score + narrative interpretation | A PHQ-9 of 12 with clear cause ≠ same as PHQ-9 of 12 with no cause |
| 5 | **2-week watch with non-pharmacological intervention FIRST** | Sleep hygiene, social prescription, physical activity, Freire circle. ISRS only if no improvement after 2-4 weeks |
| 6 | **REGISTRO VASCONCELOS**: document what was NOT prescribed and WHY | Quaternary prevention: protecting the patient from overdiagnosis |

## PART III: SOPHISTICATING CLASSICAL SEMIOLOGY

### 3.1. What Classical Semiology Misses

| Classical semiology | What it captures | What it MISSES |
|--------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Inspection | Color, shape, asymmetry | Fear on the patient's face |
| Palpation | Masses, tenderness, temperature | The flinch from social touch deprivation |
| Auscultation | Heart sounds, crackles, bruits | The sigh between sentences |
| Percussion | Resonance, dullness | The rhythm of the patient's speech |

### 3.2. The Vasconcelos Semiological Complement

| Vasconcelos sign | What the physician observes | What it reveals |
|-----------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **The Hesitation Sign** | Patient starts sentence, stops, restarts | What was NOT said is more important than what was said |
| **The Door Handle Moment** | Patient reveals real concern while leaving | High-status environment suppressed the real complaint |
| **The Partner Glance** | Patient looks at companion before answering | Power dynamics; possible coercion or dependency |
| **The List Phenomenon** | Patient brings written list of 10+ complaints | Anxiety about being heard; low trust in being listened to |
| **The Medication Display** | Patient brings all medications in a bag (unprompted) | Cry for help — polypharmacy distress |
| **The Euphemism** | Patient says "I'm just tired" or "I haven't been myself" | Depression, abuse, or existential suffering coded in social language |
| **The Google Print** | Patient brings internet research | Fear of something specific but afraid to name it |
| **The Chronic Returner** | Same patient, same complaint, third visit | Previous consultations failed to address the REAL issue |
| **The Silent Tears** | Patient cries without words during examination | Total pain (physical + emotional + spiritual + social) |
| **The Refusal Sign** | Patient refuses examination of specific area | History of abuse, trauma, or dysmorphic distress |

### 3.3. How This Changes Diagnosis

| Scenario | Classical approach | Vasconcelos approach |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Patient with chronic headache, normal neuro exam | "Tension headache. Paracetamol. Return PRN" | HESITATION SIGN observed. ESCUTA 120s reveals: caregiver for mother with Alzheimer's + husband lost job. Context: burnout, not headache. Treatment: social referral + support group, NOT analgesic |
| Elderly patient with fatigue | "Anemia workup. CBC, ferritin, B12" | MEDICATION DISPLAY: 14 medications in a bag. Treatment: deprescription (Beers criteria) before any new test |
| Young woman with vague abdominal pain, 4th visit | "More imaging. Refer to GI" | CHRONIC RETURNER pattern triggered. ESCUTA reveals domestic violence (EUPHEMISM: "things at home are complicated"). Treatment: safety plan, social services, NOT colonoscopy |

## PART V: PALLIATIVE CARE — THE DUTY OF PRESENCE

### 5.1. Cicely Saunders and Total Pain

Dame Cicely Saunders (1918-2005), founder of the modern hospice movement and St Christopher's Hospice, coined the concept of **TOTAL PAIN**:

> "Total pain is the suffering that encompasses all of a person's physical, psychological, social, spiritual, and practical struggles."

### 5.2. The Lie: "There Is Nothing More We Can Do"

This phrase — spoken by physicians worldwide — is the ULTIMATE failure of medical culture:

| The lie | The truth |
|---------|-----------|
| "There is nothing more we can do" | There is ALWAYS something you can do |
| "We've exhausted all options" | You've exhausted all PHARMACEUTICAL options. Human options are infinite |
| "You should consider hospice" (said as abandonment) | "I will stay with you through this" (said as commitment) |

### 5.3. The Vasconcelos Protocol for Palliative Care

| What you can ALWAYS do | Vasconcelos pillar | Cost |
|------------------------|-------------------|------|
| SIT with the patient | STATUS: descend to human level | R$ 0 |
| LISTEN to what they need (not what you think they need) | ESCUTA extended | R$ 0 |
| ASK about their fears | MODELO: "What frightens you most?" | R$ 0 |
| MANAGE their symptoms aggressively | EXAME + evidence-based palliation | Variable |
| COORDINATE their care so they don't fall through cracks | TRANSMISSAO + FLUXO VIVO | R$ 0 |
| INCLUDE the family | ENSEMBLE | R$ 0 |
| SAY THEIR NAME when you enter the room | STATUS: recognition of personhood | R$ 0 |
| TELL THE TRUTH with compassion | SPIKES + status descent | R$ 0 |
| PROMISE presence, not cure | ACORDO: "I will be here" | R$ 0 |
| DOCUMENT their wishes | REGISTRO VASCONCELOS: ACORDO field | R$ 0 |

### 5.4. The Vasconcelos Principle of Palliative Care

> **"When you say 'there is nothing more we can do,' you are describing YOUR limitation — not the patient's situation. A physician's greatest technology is not a machine. It is their presence."**
