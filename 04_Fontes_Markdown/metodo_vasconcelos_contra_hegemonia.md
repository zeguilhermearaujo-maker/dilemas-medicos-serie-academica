## THESIS

Medical fake news is not the disease. It is a SYMPTOM.

The disease is **medical hermeticism** — the systematic use of inaccessible language, ritualized authority, and institutional exclusion that has severed the relationship between medicine and the people it was created to serve.

When medicine becomes a fortress, the people build their own healers from whatever materials are available — including lies, political ideologies, and conspiracy theories.

**The cure for fake news in medicine is not MORE authority. It is MORE accessibility. More humanity. More translation. More presence.**

        ↓

        ↓

        ↓

### 1.3. The Paradox

The physician who insists on speaking in Latin-derived terminology to maintain authority is the same physician who complains that patients believe in fake news. **The physician IS part of the problem.** The hermetic language that protects the medical pedestal is the same language that drives patients toward misinformation.

## PART II: THE POLITICIZATION OF SCIENCE AS SYMPTOM

### 2.1. How Did We Get Here?

| Phase | What happened | How medicine contributed |
|-------|--------------|------------------------|
| **Pre-internet** | Knowledge asymmetry was absolute. The physician was the ONLY source | The pedestal was stable because there was no alternative |
| **Early internet** (2000s) | Patients began searching symptoms online | Medicine responded with CONTEMPT — "Dr. Google" became a pejorative |
| **Social media** (2010s) | Misinformation became shareable, viral, emotionally engaging | Medicine responded with MORE jargon, MORE authority, MORE distance |
| **COVID-19** (2020-2021) | Trust crisis exploded. Vaccines became political identity markers | Medicine failed to TRANSLATE. Politicians translated INSTEAD |
| **Post-COVID** (2022-present) | Anti-science is now organized POLITICAL movement, not random confusion | Medicine STILL hasn't changed its communication paradigm |

### 2.2. The Identity Trap

When science becomes inaccessible, political affiliation fills the epistemological vacuum:

| What the person believes | What it ACTUALLY signals | Why medicine is complicit |
|-------------------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| "Vaccines don't work" | "I don't trust institutions that excluded me" | The institution never spoke to them in their language |
| "Ivermectin cures COVID" | "I trust THIS community more than THAT institution" | The institution offered data without relationship |
| "Natural remedies are better" | "I want control over my own body" | Medicine took control away through paternalism |
| "Doctors only want money" | "I have been treated as a transaction, not a person" | The 7-minute consultation proved them right |

### 2.3. The Vasconcelos Counter-Strategy

The solution is NOT to fight misinformation with MORE information. The solution is to fight misinformation with **BETTER RELATIONSHIPS.**

| Traditional approach | Vasconcelos approach |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| "You're wrong. Here are the facts" | "I understand why you might think that. Tell me what you've heard" (ESCUTA) |
| "Trust the science" | "Let me explain what this study actually showed, in a way that makes sense for YOUR life" (FEYNMAN-VASCONCELOS) |
| Infographic with statistics | Conversation with eye contact, at equal status (STATUS DESCENT) |
| Fact-checking website | Freire Circle: community dialogue where people construct understanding TOGETHER |
| Correcting the patient | Asking the patient: "What do YOU think is happening?" (MODELO) and building FROM their understanding |

## PART III: THE BODY WITHOUT ORGANS — DELEUZE-GUATTARI IN THE CLINIC

### 3.1. Why This Matters for Medicine

Gilles Deleuze and Felix Guattari, building on Antonin Artaud's radical vision, proposed the concept of the **Body without Organs** (BwO) — a body understood not as a collection of organ-systems, but as a FIELD OF INTENSITIES, flows, thresholds, and potentials.

The biomedical model does the OPPOSITE: it reduces the person to a collection of organ-systems, each assigned to a specialist, each treated in isolation.

| Biomedical body | Deleuzian body (BwO) | Vasconcelos integration |
|----------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Collection of organs | Field of intensities | The person is not their organs |
| Each organ = one specialty | Flows cross all boundaries | DESCOMPARTIMENTALIZAÇÃO: against fragmentation |
| Disease = organ malfunction | Illness = disruption of flow | CONTEXTO: what disrupted the person's life? |
| Treatment = fix the organ | Care = restore the flow | ACORDO: what matters to the person? |
| The body belongs to medicine | The body belongs to the person | The DIAGNOSIS belongs to the person |

### 3.2. Clinical Application

When a physician encounters a "patient with diabetes, hypertension, depression, and chronic pain" — the biomedical model sees 4 diseases in 4 organ systems requiring 4 specialists and 12 medications.

The Vasconcelos-Deleuzian approach sees ONE PERSON whose flow of life has been disrupted. The question is not "which organs are failing?" but **"what happened to this person that disrupted their life?"** The answer may be: grief, poverty, loneliness, abuse, or exhaustion — none of which are organ diseases.

## PART IV: REHABILITATING THE PSYCHE — JUNG, FREUD, LACAN IN MEDICINE

### 4.1. The Great Exclusion

| Thinker | Core contribution | WHY medicine excluded it | WHY the Vasconcelos Method rehabilitates it |
|---------|------------------|------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Freud** | The unconscious drives behavior. Denial, repression, somatization are REAL | "Not evidence-based" (but: placebo effect IS psychoanalytic) | Somatization is one of the most common presentations in primary care. Without Freud, we cannot understand WHY the body speaks |
| **Jung** | Archetypes, shadow, individuation. The physician has a shadow too | "Too mystical" (but: physician burnout IS a shadow problem) | The physician's shadow — unprocessed emotions, fear of death, need for control — affects EVERY consultation |
| **Lacan** | Language structures reality. The signifier creates the illness | "Too abstract" (but: the WORD "cancer" kills faster than the disease) | The diagnosis IS a signifier. How a physician SAYS "you have cancer" determines how the patient LIVES with cancer |
| **Rogers** | Unconditional positive regard. The relationship IS the therapy | Already partially integrated (motivational interviewing) | The ESCUTA protocol is Rogerian. The MODELO question is person-centered therapy |
| **Winnicott** | The "good enough" holding environment | "Pediatrics only" | Every patient needs a holding environment. The consultation SHOULD be one |

### 4.2. The Vasconcelos Principle of Psychotherapeutic Integration

> **"Every medical consultation IS a psychotherapeutic encounter. The question is not WHETHER the physician practices psychotherapy. The question is whether they practice it WELL or BADLY — because they practice it regardless."**

When a physician tells a patient "you have an incurable disease," they have just performed a psychotherapeutic intervention — a BAD one. They have inscribed a signifier (Lacan) into the patient's symbolic order that may accelerate the very decline they diagnosed.

## PART V: THE VIOLENCE OF MEDICAL LANGUAGE

### 5.1. Terms That Must Be Abolished

| Medical term | What it ACTUALLY says to the patient | WHY it is violence | Vasconcelos replacement |
|-------------|--------------------------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| **"Paciente poliqueixoso"** | "You complain too much. Your suffering annoys us" | It is a confession of the physician's incompetence disguised as a diagnosis of the patient | "Patient presenting multiple concerns — requiring comprehensive contextual assessment" |
| **"Non-compliant patient"** | "You don't obey. You are a bad patient" | It blames the patient for the SYSTEM's failure to communicate, to engage, to negotiate | "Patient with unmet adherence needs — medication regimen/treatment plan requires renegotiation" |
| **"Incurable"** | "There is nothing to be done. Give up" | There is ALWAYS something to be done (Saunders). Palliative care IS care | "Condition requiring ongoing management and comprehensive support" |
| **"Frequent flyer"** (ER term) | "You keep coming back. Go away" | The patient returns because the PREVIOUS visit did not address the real issue | "Patient with recurrent care needs — root cause analysis indicated" |
| **"Hysteria"** / "Functional" | "It's in your head. It's not real" | Somatization IS real. The body speaks what the mouth cannot (Freud) | "Somatic presentation — psychosocial assessment required" |
| **"Poor historian"** | "You can't tell your own story. You are incompetent" | Perhaps the physician was a POOR LISTENER | "Patient communication requires adaptation — consider health literacy, language, cognitive status" |

### 5.2. White Coat Syndrome — The Proof That the Pedestal Is Pathological

White coat hypertension (prevalence: 15-30% of diagnosed hypertensives) is the CLEAREST evidence that the physician's presence ITSELF can cause disease.

| What medicine says | What the Vasconcelos Method says |
|-------------------|--------------------------------|
| "It's just white coat syndrome. Take the readings at home" | The fact that YOUR PRESENCE raises someone's blood pressure by 20-30 mmHg is a measurement of the STATUS VIOLENCE you carry. It is not the patient's problem. It is YOURS |

## PART VIII: THE DIAGNOSIS BELONGS TO THE PERSON

### 8.1. Reformulating the Concept

The diagnosis is not a label that the physician attaches to the patient. It is a TOOL that must serve the patient's well-being. If the word causes more harm than the condition it describes, the word must change.

| Traditional practice | Vasconcelos practice |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| "You have cancer" (sentence) | "We found something that needs treatment. Let me explain exactly what it is and what we can do" (invitation to partnership) |
| "You have Alzheimer's" (death sentence) | "We're seeing changes in your memory. There are things we can do RIGHT NOW to maintain your quality of life" (action plan) |
| "Your condition is chronic and progressive" (hopelessness) | "This is something we'll manage together over time. I'll be with you at every step" (commitment) |

### 8.2. The Person Is Not a Disease

The Vasconcelos Method insists that language shapes reality (per Lacan). Therefore:

| DO NOT say | DO say |
|-----------|--------|
| "The diabetic in Room 3" | "Mrs. Santos, who is living with diabetes" |
| "The schizophrenic" | "Mr. Silva, who experiences psychotic episodes" |
| "The terminal patient" | "Mr. Oliveira, who is in palliative care" |
| "The non-compliant patient" | "Ms. Costa, whose treatment plan needs redesign" |
